2.2 — Research Gap Analysis

The synthesis of zinc oxide nanoparticles using plant-based extracts has attracted considerable attention over the past decade due to its environmental advantages over conventional chemical synthesis routes. Studies by [Ref: Sharma et al., 2019] and [Ref: Osei-Mensah, 2021] demonstrate promising antibacterial properties in biomedical applications.

However, a significant gap exists in the literature regarding the systematic optimisation of synthesis parameters — specifically plant extract concentration, pH levels, and reaction temperature — for African plant species. The majority of existing studies focus on Asian botanical sources, leaving the antibacterial efficacy of locally-sourced Ghanaian plant extracts largely unexplored.
